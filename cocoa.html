Why Is Chocolate So Expensive?

Severe drought in West Africa — responsible for ~70% of global cocoa supply — devastated harvests in 2023–2024. El Niño weather patterns hit Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana hardest. EU chocolate prices rose 18% in 2025, making it the highest food price increase category. Global average retail chocolate prices have surged in response to raw cocoa costs.

West Africa Supply Risk

The extreme concentration of cocoa production in West Africa — particularly Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana — creates systemic supply vulnerability. El Niño-driven droughts in 2023–24 triggered the worst cocoa shortage in decades.

📈 Market Outlook 2026–2033

The global cocoa market was valued at $12.5B in 2022 and is forecast to reach $18.02B by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 4.7%. Key drivers include premiumization, health-focused cocoa products (high-flavanol), and Asia-Pacific consumption growth.

🌱 Sustainability Trends 2025–2026
Fairtrade & Rainforest Alliance certification growth
Traceable supply chain tech adoption (blockchain)
Disease-resistant cocoa variety R&D expansion
Smallholder income protection programs (ESG)
EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) compliance drive
